# principles

## How I think

1. Pivoting doesn't mean abandoning what you do. It means changing the context in which you deliver it. (See: [Has He Arrived Yet?" I Was Just Trying to Send a Jacket.](https://medium.com/@theeastperspective/has-he-arrived-yet-i-was-just-trying-to-send-a-jacket-583258529ed3))  
2. The product or service holds. What adapts is who you're selling to and how you reach them. (See: [I Fixed a Cough With Herbal Jelly From a Mall](https://medium.com/@theeastperspective/i-fixed-a-cough-with-herbal-jelly-from-a-mall-216ce10cbc92))  
3. A vague pivot stays vague until you map the process. The map is what tells you which parts actually matter. (See: [“We Don’t Have Time. We Just Need Sales.”](https://medium.com/@theeastperspective/we-dont-have-time-we-just-need-sales-i-didn-t-realise-i-was-asking-a-question-nobody-else-would-eb2a8e1fa18e))

## How I make decisions

I build toward a long arc plan. Daily adjustments serve that plan, not short-term noise. I watch both how things work internally and how people work with me, and only act on what actually moves toward the goal.
